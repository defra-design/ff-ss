The vision

Create a clear, consistent and reliable way for people to apply for funding that supports food production and protects our landscape for future generations and for staff to manage applications and support applicants.

Background

In FFCP we all need to agree what we mean by single service. The output of this work will be critical in helping us achieve this agreement.

It is the role of the single service team to show how different delivery teams will align into one over the coming years. Currently we do not have a visual representation of the user experience for a single service.

The design spike is a way of rapidly starting this work and getting agreement from all stakeholders and leadership on the intended user experience for the single service.

This work will help provide an answer to the question ‘What does the single service look like to our users?’.

The plan

In scope:

  • Defra ID
  • Eligibility
  • Application
  • Payment
  • Agreements
  • Land management
  • Personal account ( manage my account)
  • Backend office and process (internal interface)

Our goal is to give a representative and realistic end to end journey. We will prioritize showing a full end to end experience over tackling difficult details. We will document where we see difficulties.

Out of scope:

  • Detail of scheme guidance (we will use representative content)
  • Inspections and Enforcement



Method

We’ll build a working prototype in code using the gov prototyping toolkit. We’ll regularly test this with users. We’ll work in short sprints of ideation, building and testing, then iterating




Deliverables

  1. Documented user needs, team’s assumptions, knowledge gaps, and problem statements.
  2. Understanding whether our plan for the single service maps well to user expectations
  3. 2-3 show and tells to track progress. ( note: first show and tell SLT)
  4. Updated future state storyboards and service blueprint.
  5. Feed-in a single backlog for continuing this work (what we could / should do after this spike)
  6. Consider how any work emerging from this will link to the work in FFCP single service development work package (this is the technical delivery)
